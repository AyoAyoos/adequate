Design An Artificial Intelligence-Based Framework For Evaluating Adversity Quotient (AQ) In Higher Education

Presented By: Ms. Jyoti Gundesha Mudkanna (MITU21PHDT0012)
Research Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Reena Pagare
Department: Computer Science & Engineering, MIT School of Computing, MIT-ADT University, Pune.

1. Introduction & Motivation

The research focuses on a multidisciplinary approach linking Psychology, Education, and AI to foster cognitive and adversity skills. It explores the interdependence of teaching, learning, and evaluation in the educational process.

Key Concepts

  • Adaptability: The ability to adapt to changing environments (e.g., "Turning obstacles into opportunities").
  • Assessment Types:
    • Assessment for Learning: Ongoing and actionable.
    • Assessment of Learning: Grade-based.
    • Assessment as Learning: Actively involves students.

2. Problem Statement

"Develop a framework or methodology to evaluate and guide students’ career decisions in higher education, integrating the Adversity Quotient with a structured assessment scheme."

3. Adversity Quotient (AQ) Models

AQ is a measure of how one responds to adversity. It is a scientifically grounded set of tools for improving response to challenges.

CO2REA Model

  • Control: Extent to which an individual realizes they can manage a challenging situation.
  • Ownership: Extent to which the individual acknowledges the causes and consequences of challenges.
  • Origin: Degree of owning the outcomes of adversity.
  • Reach: Ability to ensure adversities do not affect other parts of essential being (health, performance, etc.).
  • Endurance: Ability to withstand the challenge and its duration.
  • Attitude: The constructive mindset and judgment used to respond to challenges.

4. Methodology of Research

The system follows a structured pipeline:

  1. Data Acquisition: Collecting constructed-response items and utilizing specific rubrics.
  2. Data Pre-processing: Noise removal and data normalization.
  3. Assessment with Neural Network: Generating classes based on Scholastic and Life Skills.
  4. Evaluation: Collecting results from the NN and teacher’s evaluation (Training Phase).
  5. Decision Making: Finding out the AQ of the individual student (Testing Phase).

5. Project Objectives

  • Objective 1: Conduct assessments and collect constructed-response items at the institution to aid analyses. (Includes Data Collection and Instrument Preparation like ARP-IHEI).
  • Objective 2: To relate Adversity Quotient and Item Response Theory in Indian Higher Education using CFA and EFA analysis.
  • Objective 3: To outline the Adversity Quotient and calculate AQ based on assessments.
  • Objective 4: To establish a novel relationship between AQ and students’ career decision-making using Bloom’s Taxonomy.
  • Objective 5: To validate and compare the proposed model with existing systems.

6. Bloom’s Taxonomy & AI Integration

The framework maps AQ traits to Bloom's Taxonomy levels to identify cognitive and affective domains.

7. Technical Implementation

The system utilizes advanced Deep Learning models:

  • BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers): Used for question classification and semantic understanding.
  • Bi-GRU (Bidirectional Gated Recurrent Unit): An advanced version of LSTM, less complex with higher accuracy.
  • Cosine Similarity & S-BERT: Applied for multilabel classification to map questions to Bloom's levels.

8. Expected Outcomes

  • Extraction of features required for Formative assessment.
  • Generation of a comprehensive dataset.
  • Designing the most suitable ARP instrument for finding adversity.
  • Designing a Framework that recommends a career for a student.
